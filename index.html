// Project Overview

Cosmic rays activate stories
about humanity's near-far future

Messages from Space is an immersive installation where cosmic rays* activate sculptural instruments and respond to each impact with light, sound, and a science fiction narrative.

At the center is Liz. She is 126 years old and has chosen the date of her death. Before she goes, she uploads her memories to an archive. Her voice threads through the installation, reflecting on a century-plus of living: the First Pandemic and Fire-Flood Years, the Second Pandemic, the great dispersal — civilization-scale events that reshaped the world she knew. The other voices belong to people in that same near-far future, speaking casually and intimately about small moments of life: a doctor, two centenarians, a young woman, an old man.

What they left in the archive is what you receive as you tune in on the walk. These are the Messages from Space. *Cosmic Rays are high-energy radiation from deep space that produce cascades of particles as they strike Earth's atmosphere.

10th annual Shenzhen-Hong Kong Biennale. Messages from Off-World, a spin-off project in VR, is developed — led by Sofy with her students at The Institute for Future Technologies at Pôle Léonard de Vinci, Paris.

Bombay Beach Biennale. FM transmission and receiver tested in an outdoor landscape for the first time.
